This is Formula 1

What is Formula 1?

Formula One, also known as F1, is a motor racing sport. Motor racing sports are said to be one of the most recent sports to be invented since they only came to existence after the invention of the first series of gasoline-fueled internal-combustion engine automobiles around the 1880s.

The FIA is the governing body for world motor sport and the federation of the world’s leading motoring organisations. Founded in 1904, with headquarters in Paris, it is a non-profit making association. It brings together 244 international motoring and sporting organisations from 146 countries on five continents. Its member clubs represent millions of motorists and their families.

Origin of Formula 1

The first edition of the modern F1 took place in 1946, and the sport is said to have originated from the European Grand Prix championships in the 1920s and 1930s.

The term ‘Formula’ stands for the various sets of regulations and standards that must be met by all the cars competing in the formula one competition.

Since the first edition of the F1 World Championship, the FIA, which is the body that governs all motorsports, has implemented several modifications to the initial pre-World War II regulations.

The goal of the initial rules was to bring about a sort of balance between supercharged and regularly aspirated automobiles.

However, some more recent modifications to the regulations specify particular dimensions and weight limitations for Formula One automobiles.

Traditionally, a Formula One vehicle must only have four wheels, and there must be minimal distances between these wheels and the rear and front bodywork.
